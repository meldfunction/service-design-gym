# Go-to-Field Readiness
## Soil Testing — Is the Ground Ready to Receive This Seed?

---

### Nature Analog
A seed dropped into unprepared soil doesn't germinate — it rots. Before a plant species propagates into a new territory, the conditions must be right: the soil pH, the moisture, the presence of mycorrhizal partners, the absence of competitive species that would crowd it out. A skilled botanist tests the soil before planting. The test is not optional.

> *"The pioneer species does not ask whether the habitat is ideal. It asks whether the minimum viable conditions are present."*

---

### Biological Principle
**Ecological succession.** Ecosystems don't jump straight to their climax state. Pioneer species — fast-growing, opportunistic — prepare the ground for what comes next. Go-to-Field readiness asks: are the pioneer conditions in place? What must already be true before the designed service can take root?

---

### What It Is
Go-to-Field Readiness is a structured assessment of whether the conditions exist for a validated service concept to be deployed and survive beyond a pilot. It tests the soil — not the seed.

Unlike a launch checklist (which asks "is the product ready?"), a readiness assessment asks "is the ecosystem ready to receive it?"

---

### When to Use
- **Go-to-Field phase** — before committing deployment resources
- After a successful prototype phase — before scaling
- When a pilot has ended and you are deciding whether to expand or hold
- When a new context or geography is being considered for replication

---

### Biomimetic Method

**The Five Soil Conditions**

Assess each condition on a 3-point scale: 🔴 Not present → 🟡 Partial → 🟢 Present

| Condition | Legacy Term | Assessment Question |
|-----------|------------|-------------------|
| **Actor alignment** | Stakeholder buy-in | Do the key actors who must change behaviour understand why and agree to the change? |
| **Root infrastructure** | Operational capacity | Do the systems, processes, and skills exist to deliver the service reliably? |
| **Signal network** | Feedback loops | Is there a mechanism for the service to sense and respond to what is happening in the field? |
| **Germination window** | Market timing | Is this the right moment — are there external forces that favour this, or that would kill it? |
| **Nutrient budget** | Funding runway | Is there enough resource to survive the establishment phase before the service becomes self-sustaining? |

**Readiness Map**
- All 🟢: Deploy with confidence
- 3+ 🟢, 1-2 🟡: Deploy with named mitigations for yellow conditions
- Any 🔴: Hold. Address the red conditions first.

**Edge Condition Assessment**
Beyond the five core conditions, ask:
1. *What would a 1-in-10 failure look like, and could we survive it?*
2. *Who is most likely to resist this after launch — and what would bring them in?*
3. *What would we need to see in the first 30 days to know this is working?*

---

### Outputs
- A soil readiness map: 5 conditions rated and annotated
- A named mitigation plan for any yellow conditions
- A 30-day signal checklist: what success looks like early
- A go/hold decision with clear rationale

---

### Facilitation Notes
- Run this with the delivery team, not just the design team — they know what's really possible
- Red conditions are not failures; they are the next sprint of work
- Revisit the readiness map every 4 weeks post-launch — conditions change

---

### Time
**Readiness workshop:** 2 hours  
**Documentation:** 45 min  
**Review cadence:** 30 min monthly post-launch

---

→ **Go-to-Field** (primary — the core deployment readiness tool)
