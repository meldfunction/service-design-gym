# Contextual Inquiry
## Niche Adaptation — Understanding Behaviour in Its Habitat

---

### Nature Analog
You cannot understand how a cuttlefish hunts by watching it in an aquarium. Its camouflage, its hunting strategy, its social dynamics are all expressions of its relationship to a specific reef ecosystem. Remove it from the context and you remove the behaviour. Place a researcher in the reef and the real behaviour becomes visible.

> *"The organism and its environment are not separate things. They are a single functional unit."*
> — Jakob von Uexküll

---

### Biological Principle
**Umwelt.** Von Uexküll proposed that every organism lives in its own subjective perceptual universe — the umwelt — shaped by its sensory apparatus and its ecological niche. You cannot understand an organism's behaviour without entering its umwelt. Contextual inquiry is an attempt to enter the human umwelt of the people you are designing for.

---

### What It Is
Contextual Inquiry is a structured observation method where you observe people **doing real tasks in their actual environment** while asking questions to understand what you are seeing.

Unlike interviews (which happen in neutral locations, after the fact) and surveys (which ask about behaviour in the abstract), contextual inquiry captures **behaviour as it actually occurs**, in the environment that shapes it.

---

### When to Use
- **Observe phase** — to understand real behaviour rather than reported behaviour
- **Adapt phase** — to test whether your prototype works in the actual context, not a simulated one
- When you suspect that what people *say* they do and what they *actually* do are different (they usually are)

---

### Biomimetic Method

**Enter the Habitat**
1. Arrange to be present while the participant does real work — not a simulation, not a demo
2. Take the role of apprentice, not expert: *"Teach me what you know. I am here to learn, not to evaluate."*
3. Bring only what a naturalist brings: a notebook, quiet attention, and genuine curiosity

**The Four Principles (adapted from Beyer & Holtzblatt)**

| Principle | Nature Analog | Practice |
|-----------|---------------|---------|
| **Context** | Study in the habitat | Observe in their real environment, not yours |
| **Partnership** | Co-species study | Treat the participant as the expert |
| **Interpretation** | Hypothesis testing | Share your interpretations and invite correction |
| **Focus** | Niche observation | Keep attention on the challenge you are studying |

**Observe and Annotate**
While observing, annotate in three streams:
- 🔵 **Behaviour**: what the person does, in sequence
- 🟢 **Workarounds**: adaptations they have made to a system that doesn't quite fit them
- 🔴 **Friction**: where the person hesitates, backtracks, improvises, or expresses frustration

**Share Your Interpretations**
Periodically pause and say: *"I noticed you [did X]. I'm interpreting that as [Y]. Is that right?"* This is the inquiry part of contextual inquiry. The participant's correction of your interpretation is often the richest data.

**Biomimicry Probes During Observation**
Ask context-specific questions:
- *"What does this process need that it doesn't currently have?"*
- *"If you could change one thing about how this works, what would take root most easily?"*
- *"What are you adapting around that you shouldn't have to adapt around?"*

---

### Outputs
- Annotated behaviour sequences: what people actually do vs. what systems expect them to do
- A workaround map — the adaptations people have developed independently (these are design insights)
- Friction log — where the environment is working against the organism
- Rich material for Pattern and Emulate stages

---

### Facilitation Notes
- Two researchers are ideal: one observes, one asks questions — avoid role confusion
- Workarounds are gold. They show you where the designed system fails and what people actually need.
- Resist fixing problems during the session — you are there to understand, not to help (yet)

---

### Time
**Per session:** 1–2 hours  
**Debrief and notes:** 30 min  
**Synthesis across sessions:** 2 hours (after 4+ sessions)

---

### Connected Stages
→ **Observe** (primary — real behaviour in real context)  
→ **Adapt** (secondary — testing in context)
