# Metabolic Strategy Canvas
## Organism Design — How Does This Thing Sustain Itself?

---

### Nature Analog
Every organism has a metabolic strategy: how it acquires energy, what it does with that energy, which relationships sustain it, and what happens when conditions change. The cactus stores water in its tissues for the dry season. The fig tree feeds a hundred species in exchange for pollination. The dandelion invests in a parachute seed that needs no particular landing spot. Each is a coherent survival strategy.

A business model is an organism's metabolic strategy. If it cannot sustain itself in its environment, it dies regardless of how elegant its design.

> *"Form ever follows function — but in living systems, function also follows form."*
> — Biomimicry Institute

---

### Biological Principle
**Metabolic efficiency.** Living organisms don't waste energy. Every calorie acquired must either fuel growth, reproduction, defence, or be stored. Organisms that expend more energy than they acquire don't survive. The Metabolic Strategy Canvas asks the same question of a service: are the energy flows — revenue, value exchange, partnership returns — sufficient to sustain the system and enable it to grow?

---

### What It Is
The Metabolic Strategy Canvas is the biomimicry equivalent of a Business Model Canvas. It maps the nine dimensions of a service's survival strategy using ecological language, surfacing assumptions about value exchange, partner dependencies, and resource flows that traditional business frameworks often obscure.

**Legacy term:** Business Model Canvas (Osterwalder & Pigneur)

---

### When to Use
- **Business Design phase** — to design the metabolic strategy of a new service
- Before investor or funder conversations — to pressure-test assumptions
- When a service is failing to sustain itself — to diagnose the metabolic breakdown
- When entering a new market or geography — to test whether the existing strategy fits the new soil

---

### Biomimetic Method

**The Nine Metabolic Dimensions**

| Biomimicry Term | Legacy Term | Core Question |
|----------------|-------------|--------------|
| **Value Organism** | Value Proposition | What does this service uniquely offer, and to whom is it perfectly adapted? |
| **Habitat Segments** | Customer Segments | Which niches does this serve? Who is the keystone user? |
| **Mutualist Partners** | Key Partners | Who gives to this and receives in return — true mutualism, not extraction? |
| **Core Capabilities** | Key Activities | What must this organism do exceptionally well to survive? |
| **Root Resources** | Key Resources | What is the irreplaceable infrastructure? What would kill us if we lost it? |
| **Dispersal Channels** | Channels | How does value reach the right habitat? What carries the seed? |
| **Symbiotic Bonds** | Customer Relationships | How do relationships deepen over time without becoming parasitic? |
| **Energy Costs** | Cost Structure | What does staying alive cost? What is fixed, what is variable? |
| **Energy Returns** | Revenue Streams | How does the system generate surplus? Is the energy equation positive? |

**Pressure-Test Questions**
After filling the canvas:
1. *If the keystone partner disappeared tomorrow, what survives?*
2. *What is the smallest viable version of this organism — the minimum to be self-sustaining?*
3. *What season is this adapted for? What happens in an unfavourable season?*
4. *Where is the parasite risk — who could extract value without contributing?*

**Mutualism vs Extraction Check**
For each partner relationship: draw an arrow of value in both directions. If arrows are one-directional, the relationship is extractive — it will either collapse or produce resentment.

---

### Outputs
- A completed 9-block Metabolic Strategy Canvas
- A mutualism check: bilateral value flows for all partner relationships
- A minimum viable organism definition
- Top 3 assumptions that require testing before investment

---

### Facilitation Notes
- Do not fill in order — start with Value Organism and Habitat Segments, then work outward
- The Energy Returns block is often the weakest — teams avoid it. Start there if the group is avoiding it.
- A canvas is a hypothesis, not a plan. Label it clearly.

---

### Time
**Canvas session:** 2–3 hours  
**Mutualism check:** 30 min  
**Assumption extraction:** 45 min
