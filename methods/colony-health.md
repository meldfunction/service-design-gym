# Colony Health Assessment
## Ecosystem Health Check — Is the Service Thriving or Just Surviving?

---

### Nature Analog
A beehive can appear productive on the outside while collapsing from within. Varroa mite infestation, queen failure, pesticide exposure, winter starvation — the hive's external behaviour masks internal distress until it is too late. Expert beekeepers assess colony health through multiple indicators: brood pattern, honey stores, worker behaviour, sound, smell. A healthy colony does not require constant intervention. It self-organises, self-regulates, and returns to stability after disturbance.

> *"A healthy ecosystem does not require management. It requires the conditions for self-regulation."*
> — Holmgren, Permaculture Principles

---

### Biological Principle
**Ecosystem health indicators.** Ecologists measure ecosystem health not through a single metric but through a portfolio of indicators: species diversity, nutrient cycling rate, soil biota density, keystone species population, edge habitat quality. No single number captures health. Colony Health Assessment takes the same multi-indicator approach to service operations.

---

### What It Is
Colony Health is a structured assessment of a live service's operational health across six dimensions. It asks not just "are the KPIs green?" but "is the system capable of self-regulating, adapting, and recovering from stress without constant management intervention?"

**Legacy term:** Service Operations Review, Customer Experience Audit, Balanced Scorecard

---

### When to Use
- **Service Operations phase** — as a regular health check cadence (monthly or quarterly)
- After a major service failure — to diagnose root causes across dimensions
- Before scaling — to confirm the system is healthy enough to survive increased load
- When staff turnover or burnout is high — often a colony health signal, not a staffing issue

---

### Biomimetic Method

**The Six Colony Health Dimensions**

| Dimension | Legacy Term | Health Signal | Distress Signal |
|-----------|------------|--------------|----------------|
| **Staff Ecosystem** | Employee Experience | Staff initiative visible; informal knowledge-sharing; low sick leave | Workarounds hidden; knowledge siloed; burnout language |
| **Process Metabolism** | Operational Efficiency | Processes fit actual workflows; exceptions handled gracefully | Frequent workarounds; staff inventing their own systems |
| **Distress Signals** | Complaints & Escalations | Complaints caught early; patterns visible; response fast | Late escalation; recurring complaints; blame cycles |
| **Adaptive Capacity** | Learning & Improvement | Team changes process based on signals; learning is documented | Same problems recur; post-mortems not done or not actioned |
| **Service Recovery** | Recovery & Resolution | Trust restored after failure; follow-up standard; empathy visible | One-size recovery; promises made but not kept; customers not followed up |
| **Succession Plan** | Knowledge Management | Roles documented; cross-training in place; service survives turnover | Single points of failure; undocumented tribal knowledge |

**Health Assessment Protocol**
For each dimension:
1. Rate current state: 🔴 Critical / 🟡 Stable / 🟢 Thriving
2. Name one health signal you are currently measuring
3. Name one distress signal that has appeared in the last 90 days
4. Identify the root system: what systemic condition is producing this health or distress?

**The Colony Question**
After rating all six: *"If the three most senior people in this service left tomorrow, would it survive?"*
- Yes → healthy succession
- Probably → moderate risk
- No → critical single-point-of-failure; address immediately

---

### Outputs
- A six-dimension health dashboard (RAG rated)
- A distress signal log for the last 90 days
- A root cause analysis for any red dimensions
- A succession risk register

---

### Facilitation Notes
- Run with frontline staff present — managers often rate health higher than the people doing the work
- Distress signals are gifts. Celebrate the person who named them.
- The Succession Plan dimension is consistently the least developed — but the most important at scale

---

### Time
**Assessment session:** 2 hours  
**Root cause analysis:** 45 min per red dimension  
**Documentation and action planning:** 1 hour

---

### Connected Stages
→ **Service Operations** (primary — the core ops health tool)  
→ **CX Strategy** (secondary — staff ecosystem dimension links to CX delivery)  
→ **Adapt** (secondary — distress signals feed back to the adapt cycle)
