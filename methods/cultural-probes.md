# Cultural Probes
## Remote Sensing — Reading What Direct Observation Misses

---

### Nature Analog
Ecologists studying deep ocean ecosystems cannot be present continuously. They deploy **autonomous sensors** — pressure gauges, temperature loggers, camera traps, acoustic monitors — that record what happens when no researcher is watching. The data that emerges reveals behaviours and patterns that only occur in the absence of human presence.

Cultural probes are the human equivalent. You send a kit into someone's life and the kit records what you cannot witness directly.

> *"The most important things happen when you are not in the room."*

---

### Biological Principle
**Remote sensing.** Trees communicate through chemical signals in the air and electrical signals in mycorrhizal networks — forms of communication that happen at scales and frequencies humans cannot directly perceive. Cultural probes are an attempt to tune into these frequencies: the quiet, ambient signals of daily lived experience.

---

### What It Is
Cultural Probes are self-documentation kits sent to participants to record their own experience over a period of time. Participants document their world in response to prompts — through photos, drawings, diary entries, found objects, maps, or voice notes.

The point is not to gather data *about* people. It is to receive an artifact *from* people — something that carries the texture and logic of their experience.

---

### When to Use
- **Observe phase** — to gather material from lives you cannot directly enter
- **Pattern phase** — to understand the ambient rhythms and rituals that observation sessions miss
- When the relevant experience is private, domestic, or spatially distributed
- When participants can articulate more through making than through talking

---

### Biomimetic Method

**Design the Kit**

Each probe kit should contain 5–8 instruments. Choose from:

| Instrument | Nature Analog | What It Captures |
|------------|--------------|-----------------|
| Disposable camera / phone prompt | Camera trap | Spontaneous, unguarded moments |
| Diary with open prompts | Migration log | Daily rhythm and emotional texture |
| Map with stickers | Territory marking | Spatial experience of the ecosystem |
| Postcard to a stranger | Pheromone signal | What they would communicate if there were no consequences |
| Finish-this-sentence cards | Birdsong variation | Language and metaphor they use for their experience |
| Draw-what-you-feel | Nest construction | Non-verbal expression of internal state |
| Found object: "something that represents how you feel about X" | Totemic signal | Symbolic relationship to the system |

**Write the Prompts**
Prompts should be:
- Open enough to allow surprise
- Specific enough to focus on the ecosystem you are studying
- Evocative, not clinical — *"draw the shape of your day"* not *"describe your daily routine"*

**Distribute and Let Go**
Return after an agreed period (3–10 days). Do not follow up, explain, or guide. The probe should work without you.

**Receive the Artefacts**
Do not immediately analyse. First, receive. Lay everything out and spend 30 minutes just being with the material before you begin to interpret. What surprises you? What disturbs you? What is absent?

**Interpret: Read for Signals**
- What recurs across participants?
- What is unique — the edge signal that doesn't fit the pattern?
- What do the artefacts tell you that an interview never could?

---

### Outputs
- A collection of participant-generated artefacts
- "I notice…" observations about what the artefacts contain
- Edge signals — the unexpected, idiosyncratic material that breaks your assumptions
- Rich material for Pattern and Emulate stages

---

### Facilitation Notes
- Brief participants on the spirit, not the method — "we want to understand what your life actually feels like, not what you think we want to hear"
- Debrief sessions with participants are often the richest part — ask "why did you choose this?" not "what does this mean?"
- Treat the artefacts with care — they are gifts

---

### Time
**Kit design:** 2–3 hours  
**Participant briefing:** 20 min per person  
**Probe period:** 5–10 days  
**Debrief per participant:** 30–45 min  
**Synthesis:** 2 hours

---

### Connected Stages
→ **Observe** (primary — gathering ambient, hard-to-access material)  
→ **Pattern** (secondary — recurring signals across participants)
