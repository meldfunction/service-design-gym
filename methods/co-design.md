# Co-design Sessions
## Co-evolution — Design as Mutualism, Not Extraction

---

### Nature Analog
The fig tree and the fig wasp have co-evolved for 60 million years. Neither could survive without the other. The wasp pollinates the fig; the fig feeds the wasp's larvae. This is not exploitation — it is **mutualism**: a relationship that makes both parties more capable over time.

Most design processes are extractive: we take knowledge from communities, process it in our studios, and return a finished product. Co-design inverts this. It is a **mutualist practice** — communities bring expertise in their own lives, designers bring fluency in shaping possibility, and together they create something neither could alone.

> *"Those closest to the problem are closest to the solution."*

---

### Biological Principle
**Symbiogenesis.** Lynn Margulis demonstrated that eukaryotic cells — the foundation of all complex life — emerged from the merger of previously separate organisms. Complexity arises from genuine combination, not from one organism dominating another.

---

### What It Is
Co-design is collaborative authorship. Participants are not *subjects* of research or *users* of outputs — they are **co-authors of the design itself**. They hold design authority, not just advisory roles.

This is not a focus group. It is not user testing. It is a structured creative process where community knowledge shapes the design space as directly as professional design skill does.

---

### When to Use
- **Emulate phase** — to develop insights and HMWs with the people most affected
- **Prototype phase** — to generate concepts with community co-authors rather than for them
- **Adapt phase** — to test and iterate concepts with the people who know the ecosystem best
- Whenever the design team lacks lived experience of the challenge

---

### Biomimetic Method

**Prepare the Conditions**
1. Recruit participants who represent the full diversity of the ecosystem — not just the most articulate or most accessible
2. Design for the participants' environment, not yours — hold sessions in their spaces, at their times, using their languages
3. Share your current understanding openly before the session — co-design requires transparent starting conditions

**Open the Session: Name the Contract**
Establish the co-authorship contract explicitly:
- *"You bring expertise in your own life. We bring design fluency. Together we are authoring something new."*
- Be explicit about what will and won't be decided in this room
- Name the power differential — and what you are doing to address it

**Generative Phase: Parallel Experiments**
Run multiple small parallel activities — not one large discussion:
- **Making** (draw, build, map) — bypasses verbal dominance
- **Sorting** (organise, prioritise, cluster) — surfaces hidden structure
- **Storifying** (narrate a future experience) — generates rich service concepts

**Convergence: What Takes Root?**
1. Share outputs across groups — what patterns recur?
2. Let participants mark what resonates and what feels false
3. Synthesis belongs to the whole group, not just the designers

**Close: Accountability Forward**
1. Share what you heard and what you will do with it
2. Establish how participants will be kept informed of outcomes
3. Be honest about constraints — false promises break mutualism

---

### Outputs
- Insights generated *by* affected people, not *about* them
- Service concepts that carry community logic, not just design logic
- A relationship of accountability to participants
- Material for your Emulate and Prototype stages

---

### Facilitation Notes
- Facilitators should be skilled in holding power — not just running activities
- Multi-language sessions are richer, not harder — build in translation as part of the design
- The most important contributions often come quietly — structure activities that don't reward extroversion
- If you extract knowledge and disappear, you have not co-designed. You have deceived.

---

### Time
**Preparation:** 2–4 hours  
**Session:** 2–3 hours  
**Synthesis and feedback to participants:** 1–2 hours

---

### Connected Stages
→ **Emulate** (primary — generating insights and HMWs together)  
→ **Prototype** (secondary — generating and testing concepts together)
