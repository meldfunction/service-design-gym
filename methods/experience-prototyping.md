# Experience Prototyping
## Germination Test — Does This Seed Take Root?

---

### Nature Analog
A seed doesn't wait for the perfect soil report before germinating. It tests the ground through germination itself — sending out a radical, spending stored energy, and reading the environment's response. If conditions are wrong, it dies cheaply. If right, it commits resources to growth.

> *"Fail fast, fail cheap, fail forward."*  
> — Nature, continuously, for 3.8 billion years

---

### Biological Principle
**Minimum viable organism.** In evolutionary biology, organisms don't build full complexity before entering an environment — they start with the minimum viable form and elaborate from feedback. The prototype *is* the research instrument.

---

### What It Is
Experience Prototyping tests **service moments, not products**. Instead of building a complete system, you simulate the critical interaction at the lowest possible fidelity that still produces a real human response.

The key word is *experience* — you are testing what it **feels like** to be inside this service, not whether the technology works.

---

### When to Use
- **Prototype phase** — primary method for testing concepts from your Emulate stage
- **Adapt phase** — refine based on what the prototype reveals
- When you need to test an assumption about human behaviour before committing resources
- When the concept is hard to describe — show it instead

---

### Biomimetic Method

**Choose Your Prototype Type**
Select the form that matches what you need to learn:

| Form | Nature Analog | Good For |
|------|--------------|----------|
| Role-play / acting | Mimic behaviour | Testing interactions and relationships |
| Physical mock-up | Nest construction | Testing spatial or tactile service moments |
| Paper interface | Leaf prototype | Testing information flows and decisions |
| Scenario video | Pheromone trail | Testing whether others understand the concept |
| Bodystorming | Pack behaviour | Testing how multiple actors move together |

**Build**
1. Identify the **one moment** that most depends on your HMW being true
2. Build the minimum version of that moment — strip everything that isn't essential
3. Make it real enough to provoke genuine response, not theoretical reaction

**Test**
1. Find 3–5 people who live in the relevant ecosystem (users, practitioners, affected community members)
2. Put them inside the experience — don't explain it, let them encounter it
3. Watch where they pause, mis-step, relax, or break the prototype's logic
4. Ask *"What were you thinking when…"* — not *"Did you like it?"*

**Read the Signals**
- Where did the prototype hold? (keep)
- Where did it break? (redesign or kill)
- What did people do that you didn't predict? (this is the richest signal)

---

### Outputs
- A clear answer to one specific assumption
- A list of behaviours you didn't predict
- A refined or killed concept — both are success
- Evidence to bring back to your Emulate stage

---

### Facilitation Notes
- Protect the experience — observers should not intervene, correct, or explain
- Low fidelity is a feature, not a bug — rough prototypes invite honest response; polished ones invite politeness
- Run multiple prototypes in the same session — nature runs parallel experiments, not sequential ones

---

### Time
**Preparation:** 60 min  
**Testing session:** 30–45 min per participant  
**Synthesis:** 45 min

---

### Connected Stages
→ **Prototype** (primary)  
→ **Adapt** (secondary — what to change based on what you learned)
