# Habitat Experience Map
## Migration Tracking — How Does the Organism Move Through the Ecosystem?

---

### Nature Analog
Ecologists tracking salmon migration don't just study the fish at spawning. They follow the entire journey: the ocean feeding grounds, the river mouth, the upstream migration, the spawning act, the post-spawn death that feeds the forest. Each phase of the journey is shaped by different conditions, different threats, different energy demands. Understanding the full migration — not just the destination — is how you understand the species.

A customer journey is a migration through a service ecosystem. Map the whole journey, or you only know part of the organism.

> *"You cannot understand a migratory species by studying only where it arrives."*

---

### Biological Principle
**Habitat corridors.** Ecologists design habitat corridors to ensure that organisms can move safely between feeding grounds, breeding grounds, and shelter. Breaks in the corridor — roads, urban development, hostile terrain — are where animals die. The Habitat Experience Map finds the breaks in the service corridor: the moments where the user's journey becomes hostile, fragmented, or fails.

---

### What It Is
The Habitat Experience Map is the biomimicry equivalent of a Customer Journey Map. It tracks a user's experience across five phases of a service encounter, mapping actions, feelings, friction points, backstage processes, and health signals at each phase.

**Legacy term:** Customer Journey Map, Experience Map

---

### When to Use
- **CX Strategy phase** — to map the current state before redesigning
- **Service Operations phase** — to identify where backstage systems are causing frontstage failure
- **Prototype phase** — to identify which moments most need prototyping
- Whenever you need to align a team around what a user actually experiences

---

### Biomimetic Method

**The Five Migration Phases**

| Phase | Nature Analog | Service Equivalent |
|-------|--------------|-------------------|
| **Approach** | Pre-migration gathering | Discovery, consideration, first contact |
| **Entry** | River mouth — transition to a new habitat | Onboarding, first transaction, sign-up |
| **Core** | Open ocean feeding | The core value delivery — the primary reason for the relationship |
| **Stress** | Obstacle migration — dams, rapids | Failure moments, complaints, friction encounters |
| **Return** | Seasonal return or new migration | Repeat use, referral, churn, or renewal |

**The Five Observation Rows**

For each phase, map:

| Row | What to capture |
|-----|----------------|
| **What they do** (Actions) | The sequence of steps the user takes |
| **How it feels** (Feelings) | The emotional texture — not just positive/negative but specific |
| **Friction** | Where they hesitate, backtrack, give up, or express frustration |
| **Backstage** | What the organisation is doing (or not doing) behind the scenes that shapes this moment |
| **Health signal** | The leading indicator that tells you if this phase is healthy — the metric that matters |

**Reading the Map**
After filling:
- *Where are the longest friction rows?* These are your habitat breaks.
- *What backstage processes are creating frontstage pain?* These are your invisible dams.
- *What health signals are you not currently measuring?* These are your blind spots.
- *Where does the journey lose the most users?* This is your most critical corridor break.

---

### Outputs
- A completed 5×5 Habitat Experience Map
- Annotated friction points with severity ratings
- A backstage process list linked to frontstage failure moments
- A health signal dashboard: 5 metrics (one per phase) worth measuring

---

### Facilitation Notes
- Run with both frontline staff and customers in the room if possible — their maps will be different, and the difference is the data
- Feelings rows are hard for operational teams — push past "frustrated" and "happy" to the specific texture
- Health signals are often missing entirely. Build the habit of asking: what would we measure to know this phase is working?

---

### Time
**Mapping session:** 2–3 hours  
**Backstage process linkage:** 45 min  
**Health signal selection:** 30 min

---
